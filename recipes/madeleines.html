Madeleines

Description

What is a madeleine?

Madeleine cookies? Madeleine cake? What the heck are they, right?

I'm going to clear it up for you right now

Although many think that madeleines are French shell shaped cookies, they are actually CAKE

The batter is typically baked in a shell-shaped mold and the finished product is often decorated with confectioners' Sugar

These delicate little shell shaped sponge cakes are sweet, buttery, and so DELICIOUS!

Ingredients

  • 1 stick (4 ounces) unsalted butter, melted until browned
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons lemon zest, finely grated
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 and 1/4 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3 large eggs, at room temperature
  • >3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup confectioners sugar, sifted, optional

Steps

  1. Cut the butter in tablespoons and place them in a heavy saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Once the butter has melted, reduce the heat to low and continue cooking, until the solids sink to the bottom of the pan and turn golden brown, about 10 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and pour the browned butter into a small bowl
  4. Stir the vanilla and lemon zest into the butter, then set aside to cool.
  5. Whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt
  6. Beat the eggs at medium speed while gradually adding the sugar
  7. Fold in the flour mixture in three additions, stirring just until combined
  8. Fold in the butter mixture
  9. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 4 hours
  10. preheat the oven to 375 degrees (F)
  11. Brush the molds of your madeleine pan with butter, then lightly dust with flour
  12. Spoon level scoops of the batter into the center of each mold.
  13. Bake for 12 minutes, or until the "bellies" have risen and they're golden brown
  14. Dust with confectioners' sugar and serve warm, with coffee or tea.
